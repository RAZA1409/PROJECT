About the Project

"Endangered Earth" is a project dedicated to raising awareness about endangered species across the globe. We aim to educate people on the importance of wildlife conservation and promote actions that help protect the most vulnerable animals.

Endangered Animals

Bengal Tiger

The Bengal tiger, a majestic and powerful predator, is one of the most iconic species on Earth.

Population: Less than 2,500

Habitat: India, Bangladesh, Nepal, Bhutan

Black Rhino

The Black Rhino is critically endangered, known for its two horns on the skull and a hooked upper lip.

Population: Around 5,000

Habitat: Southern and Eastern Africa

Giant Panda

The giant panda, known for its distinctive black-and-white fur, is one of the world's most beloved animals.

Population: Around 1,864

Habitat: Central China

Sea Turtle

Sea turtles are critically endangered due to pollution, accidental capture in fishing gear, and the destruction of nesting beaches. Conservation groups are working hard to protect their habitats and reduce bycatch.

Sumatran Orangutan

The Sumatran Orangutan is critically endangered due to habitat loss from deforestation and illegal palm oil plantations. These primates are crucial to the rainforest ecosystem as seed dispersers.

Vaquita

The vaquita, a small porpoise species found in the Gulf of California, is critically endangered due to bycatch in illegal gillnet fishing. It is one of the rarest marine mammals on earth.

Snow Leopard

Snow leopards are found in the high mountain ranges of Central Asia. They are threatened by poaching for their fur and bones, human-wildlife conflict, and habitat loss.

Blue Whale

The Blue Whale, the largest animal on Earth, is endangered due to commercial whaling, ship strikes, and habitat disturbance. Conservation efforts focus on protecting them from human-related threats.
