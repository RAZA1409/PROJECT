Introduction

The Rafflesia Flower, known scientifically as Rafflesia arnoldii, is one of the most extraordinary flowers in the world. It holds the title of being the largest flower, with some specimens reaching over 3 feet in diameter. Despite its beauty, the flower emits a foul odor, earning it the nickname "corpse flower."

Characteristics

Rafflesia is a parasitic plant, lacking leaves, stems, and roots. It grows inside a host vine, emerging only when it's ready to bloom. The bloom itself lasts for just a few days before wilting. The flower’s odor, resembling decaying flesh, attracts carrion flies that aid in its pollination.

Habitat

This rare flower is native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia, specifically in parts of Indonesia, Malaysia, Thailand, and the Philippines. It thrives in humid, low-lying forests, but deforestation and habitat destruction pose serious threats to its survival.

Conservation Status

The Rafflesia is currently listed as Endangered. The destruction of rainforests and over-collection have drastically reduced its population. Conservation efforts are focused on protecting its natural habitat and supporting sustainable tourism that helps raise awareness about this unique species.
