Introduction

The Indian Bael, scientifically known as Aegle marmelos, is a sacred tree in India, revered for its medicinal properties and religious significance. It is commonly known as Bael or Bilva and is valued for its fruit, which is used in traditional medicine and religious offerings.

Medicinal Properties

Bael is highly regarded for its medicinal value, especially in Ayurveda. The fruit pulp is used to treat digestive issues like constipation, diarrhea, and indigestion. Bael is also known for its anti-inflammatory, antiviral, and antioxidant properties, making it beneficial in treating a variety of ailments, including respiratory and skin disorders.

Habitat

Indian Bael is native to the Indian subcontinent and thrives in tropical and subtropical regions. It can be found in dry forests and plains across India, Nepal, Sri Lanka, and Southeast Asia. The tree is resilient, capable of withstanding extreme weather conditions, including drought and high temperatures.

Conservation Status

Although Indian Bael is not currently classified as endangered, it faces habitat loss due to deforestation and land-use changes. Efforts are being made to conserve this important species by encouraging its cultivation in home gardens, farms, and protected areas. The growing demand for its fruit in herbal medicine has also led to increased awareness of its importance.
