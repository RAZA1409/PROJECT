Bengal Tiger (Panthera tigris tigris)

The Bengal tiger, a majestic and powerful predator, is one of the most iconic species on Earth. It inhabits the dense forests and mangrove swamps of India, Bangladesh, Bhutan, and Nepal.

Physical Characteristics

Bengal tigers are known for their striking orange coats with black stripes. They are the largest of all tiger subspecies, with males weighing up to 500 pounds (227 kg) and measuring up to 10 feet (3 meters) in length from head to tail.

Habitat and Distribution

Bengal tigers are found primarily in India, with smaller populations in Bangladesh, Nepal, Bhutan, and Myanmar. Their habitats range from tropical rainforests to dry forests, grasslands, and mangrove swamps.

Conservation Status

Bengal tigers are classified as Endangered by the IUCN Red List, with fewer than 2,500 individuals left in the wild. They face threats from habitat loss, poaching, and conflicts with humans.
